Introduction

Context, analytics, and redesign scope

The Focalpay mobile customer app connects shoppers directly to the store — browse, scan, pay, and collect. The product existed when I joined, but analytics showed two serious drop-off problems: 47% of users abandoned onboarding before completing it, and checkout completion was significantly below industry benchmarks for mobile retail apps.

I led the redesign of both journeys — onboarding and checkout — across iOS and Android. The goal was not just to reduce steps, but to make the experience feel native, personal, and worth coming back to.

My Responsibilities

  • User research and journey mapping for the B2C shopping experience
  • Redesigning onboarding flow to reduce drop-off
  • Rethinking checkout interaction design for mobile-first users
  • A/B testing checkout variants with real users
  • Delivering micro-interactions and adaptive UI patterns

Discover

Losing Users at Every Step

Users were dropping off at two critical points — during onboarding and at checkout. The journey felt like it was designed for the system, not the person using it.

01

Onboarding Overload

Too many steps before the user could do anything useful. Drop-off happened within the first 3 screens.

02

Checkout Friction

The payment flow had unnecessary confirmation steps that interrupted the momentum of buying.

03

No Mobile Feel

The UI lacked micro-interactions and adaptive patterns that make mobile apps feel alive and responsive.

Research

Understanding the Shopper

I ran user interviews with 8 existing app users and conducted unmoderated remote usability sessions to identify where and why users were dropping off.

🛒

"I just want to pay and go. Why does it ask me so many things?"

Checkout had 6 steps — users expected 3 or fewer
📲

Users abandoned onboarding when asked to create an account before seeing what the app offered.

47% drop-off before completing onboarding in the original flow
✨

"It feels a bit... plain. Like a website, not an app."

Users rated the experience 5.8/10 on feel and polish before redesign

Process

Mobile-First, Test-Driven

01

Journey Mapping

Mapped the full user journey from download to first purchase, identifying every friction point and drop-off moment.

02

Onboarding Redesign

Moved account creation to after the user's first action — letting them explore before asking for commitment.

03

Checkout Simplification

Reduced checkout from 6 steps to 3 by combining confirmation screens and surfacing payment options earlier.

04

Micro-interactions

Added motion, haptics, and state transitions that make the app feel responsive and personal across iOS and Android.

05

A/B Testing

Tested two checkout variants with real users. Measured completion rate, time-to-purchase, and satisfaction score.

Reflection

What This Project Taught Me

01

Earn commitment before asking for it

Moving account creation after the first value moment reduced drop-off dramatically. Users give data when they trust you.

02

Motion is a design material

Micro-interactions aren't polish — they're communication. The right animation tells the user their action worked before any text confirms it.

03

Fewer steps, more trust

Every step we removed from checkout was a moment of doubt we eliminated. Simplicity builds confidence in the product.
