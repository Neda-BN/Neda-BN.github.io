Introduction

From Spreadsheets to a Smart Platform

In Sweden, healthcare providers regularly send surveys to municipalities to evaluate the quality of care services. But the process was slow, fragmented, and entirely manual — relying on emails, Excel spreadsheets, and shared document folders.

This project focused on designing a B2B SaaS platform that lets healthcare administrators create surveys, distribute them to municipalities, track responses in real time, and analyse results in one centralised place.

My Responsibilities

  • Lead UX/UI Designer and front-end developer (via Cursor AI)
  • Stakeholder interviews with healthcare administrators
  • Workflow analysis and full survey lifecycle mapping
  • Information architecture for a complex Swedish healthcare hierarchy
  • Designing a guided 6-step survey distribution wizard
  • Usability testing with 5 healthcare administrators
  • WCAG accessibility compliance across all screens

Discover

The Fragmented Workflow Problem

Healthcare administrators were responsible for collecting survey responses from 30+ municipalities, but there was no central system. Information was scattered across Outlook, Excel, and shared folders — creating a constant battle against missed deadlines and invisible progress.

Process

Clarity First, Complexity Second

The platform needed to handle complex Swedish healthcare hierarchies — providers, municipalities, districts, and social workers — without overwhelming users. My process focused on reducing cognitive load at every step.

01

Research & Workflow Mapping

Mapped the full survey lifecycle from creation to reporting. Identified 3 major bottlenecks: distribution, tracking, and analysis.

02

Information Architecture

Structured the platform into three clear sections — Surveys, Analysis, Reports — matching the mental model of administrators.

03

Wireframes & Hierarchy Design

Designed an accordion-based navigation system to handle the Swedish healthcare hierarchy without showing all levels at once.

04

Guided Wizard Design

Created the 6-step "Power Send" wizard to remove the friction of selecting and filtering 30+ municipalities manually.

05

Usability Testing

Tested with 5 healthcare administrators. Identified a critical issue with the "Select All" button visibility and iterated.

06

Build with Cursor AI

Used prompt engineering to build the platform with Cursor AI — guiding the AI to handle nested healthcare logic and WCAG compliance.

Hierarchy

Taming the Healthcare Hierarchy

The Swedish healthcare system has multiple levels — Provider → Municipality → District → Social Worker. Showing all of this at once created overwhelming lists that paralysed users.

LevelDescriptionDesign Solution
ProviderTop-level healthcare organisationAlways visible in persistent sidebar
Municipality30+ municipalities per providerAccordion — expand on click
DistrictSub-units within each municipalityNested accordion, one level at a time
Social WorkerIndividual respondentsShown only when district is selected

Reflection

What This Project Taught Me

01

Good UX organises complexity — it doesn't hide it

The hierarchy problem wasn't solved by removing levels. It was solved by revealing them progressively, one at a time, so each step felt manageable.

02

Guided flows build confidence in complex domains

Healthcare administrators are not tech-averse — they simply need clear guidance in high-stakes environments. The wizard gave them that confidence.

03

AI-assisted development is a design superpower

Using Cursor AI for implementation meant I could focus entirely on UX quality — using prompt engineering as a design tool to enforce consistency and accessibility at every step.
