Introduction

From Paper to Platform

Healthcare providers were spending hours every week on manual, paper-based surveys — collecting patient data, compliance information, and operational feedback through processes that hadn't changed in years.

I led the end-to-end UX/UI design of a digital platform that transformed this entirely — making surveys faster to complete, easier to analyse, and fully accessible to providers across different technical skill levels.

My Responsibilities

  • End-to-end UX/UI design from research to production-ready handoff
  • User interviews with healthcare administrators and providers
  • Designing accessible, WCAG-compliant interfaces for diverse users
  • Building complex user flows for multi-step survey processes
  • AI-assisted prototyping using Cursor AI for faster iteration
  • Full developer handoff with interaction specifications
The Problem

Paperwork Was Slowing Patient Care

Healthcare providers were frustrated. The time spent on administrative surveys was time taken away from patients. The tools they had were slow, confusing, and not built for how they actually worked.

01

Manual Processes

Paper forms and spreadsheets meant hours of data entry, duplication, and error-prone record keeping every week.

02

No Accessibility

Existing digital tools ignored accessibility standards — leaving providers with visual or motor impairments behind.

03

Complex Workflows

Multi-step surveys with branching logic were nearly impossible to navigate without making mistakes.

Design Process

Accessible by Default

Accessibility wasn't an afterthought — it was the foundation. Every design decision was made with WCAG 2.1 AA compliance in mind from the first wireframe.

01

User Interviews & Journey Mapping

Mapped the full survey workflow from assignment to submission, identifying every manual step and error-prone moment.

02

Information Architecture

Designed a clear IA for multi-step surveys with branching logic — making complex flows feel simple and linear to the user.

03

Accessibility-First UI

Designed with WCAG 2.1 AA compliance as the baseline — colour contrast, keyboard navigation, screen reader support, and focus states built in from day one.

04

AI-Assisted Prototyping

Used Cursor AI to accelerate prototyping cycles — iterating faster while maintaining design quality and consistency.

05

Production Handoff

Delivered production-ready UI with full Zeplin specs and interaction documentation — ensuring design intent was preserved through development.

Lessons Learned

What This Project Taught Me

01

Accessibility is not a feature — it's the foundation

Building WCAG compliance in from day one was easier and better than retrofitting it. Accessible design is just good design.

02

Complex logic needs simple language

Branching survey flows are inherently complex. The design job was to hide that complexity completely — so users never had to think about it.

03

AI tools accelerate without replacing judgment

Using Cursor AI to prototype faster gave me more time to think about the hard problems — not fewer. Speed is only valuable if it's pointed in the right direction.
