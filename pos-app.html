Redesigning a high-frequency point-of-sale app to make every transaction faster and more intuitive for store staff under real-world pressure.

Introduction

Speed is the Product

The Focalpay Cashier POS app is used by store staff during every transaction — dozens of times a day, under pressure, with queues forming. When the interface slows them down, the whole store slows down.

I was brought in to redesign the core checkout experience, focusing on information hierarchy, interaction patterns, and mobile-first flows that work equally well on iOS and Android.

My Responsibilities

  • Contextual research with cashiers on the store floor
  • Rethinking information hierarchy for the checkout flow
  • Designing interaction patterns and micro-interactions
  • Usability testing with store staff during live shifts
  • Figma prototyping and iterative developer handoff

Discover

Every Second Counts

Cashiers were losing time on every transaction — not because the system was broken, but because the interface forced them to think when they should just be acting.

01

Poor Hierarchy

Critical actions were buried. Cashiers had to hunt for buttons they used 50+ times a day.

02

No Platform Feel

The app ignored iOS and Android conventions — interactions felt unfamiliar and slow.

03

Error Recovery

Mistakes during checkout required multiple steps to fix, stalling the queue.

Research

Watching Cashiers Work

I conducted contextual observation sessions with cashiers during live shifts — watching where they hesitated, where they made errors, and what workarounds they had developed.

⏱️

"I know what I need to press — I just can't always find it fast enough."

Average of 3 unnecessary taps per transaction identified
📱

Cashiers used their thumbs almost exclusively — but key actions were placed at the top of the screen.

Thumb zone analysis showed 60% of actions were out of comfortable reach
🔄

"When I make a mistake, I have to start the whole thing over sometimes."

Error recovery took an average of 45 seconds — longer than the transaction itself

Process

Fast, Tested, Iterated

01

Contextual Research

Observed cashiers during live shifts. Mapped every tap, hesitation, and error in the existing flow.

02

Thumb Zone Mapping

Mapped iOS and Android thumb zones and repositioned all primary actions into the comfortable reach area.

03

Interaction Design

Designed micro-interactions and haptic feedback patterns that make the app feel fast and reliable.

04

Prototype & Test

Built mid-fi Figma prototypes and tested with 8 cashiers during actual shifts — not in a lab.

05

Stakeholder Alignment

Used iterative Figma prototypes to align developers and product managers before any code was written.

Reflection

What This Project Taught Me

01

Test where the work actually happens

Lab testing would have missed everything. Watching cashiers during real shifts revealed pain points no interview could surface.

02

Micro-interactions are not decoration

In a high-frequency app, the feeling of speed is as important as actual speed. Haptics and transitions reduced perceived wait time.

03

Platform conventions exist for a reason

Following iOS and Android thumb zone guidelines wasn't a constraint — it was the fastest path to an interface that felt natural.
